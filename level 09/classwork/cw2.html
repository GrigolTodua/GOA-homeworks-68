All About Sea Horses

Sea horses are small marine fish belonging to the genus *Hippocampus*. They are known for their unique horse-like head and curled tail.

Fun Facts

  • There are about 70 different species of sea horses.
  • Male sea horses carry the babies in a special pouch.
  • They use their tails to anchor themselves to seaweed.
  • Sea horses can change color to blend into their surroundings.

Habitat

Sea horses are usually found in shallow coastal waters among seagrasses, coral reefs, and mangroves.

Diet

Sea horses eat small crustaceans and plankton. They use their long snouts to suck in food.
